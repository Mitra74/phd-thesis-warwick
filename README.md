# Diffusion-Based Molecular Communication in Discrete Heterogeneous Environments

PhD Thesis — Mitra Rezaei, University of Warwick (2022–2026)
Supervisors: Prof. Michael Chappell & Dr. Adam Noel

## Overview
This thesis develops analytical and computational frameworks for modelling molecular communication
in heterogeneous, multi-layered biological environments. The work spans spherical and planar
geometries, addressing signal propagation between cell aggregates, drug delivery in layered tissue,
and large-scale environmental channels — unified by a common diffusion-based modelling approach
that accounts for heterogeneity across material/biological interfaces.

The thesis is structured around three core contributions, each corresponding to a peer-reviewed
publication and an accompanying code repository (linked below).

## How the chapters connect
- **Chapter 3** establishes the foundational model for diffusion-based signalling between discrete
  biological aggregates (spheroids), introducing the heterogeneous boundary treatment used throughout.
- **Chapter 4** extends this to multi-layered spherical geometries, applying the framework to a
  concrete drug delivery context and introducing inverse modelling to recover layer-specific transport parameters.
- **Chapter 5** generalises the approach to planar, multi-layered channels, demonstrating the
  framework's flexibility with a real-world environmental case study (ocean surface signal propagation).

Together, these chapters progress from idealised spherical biological signalling → applied
biomedical geometry → generalised environmental channels, forming a unified treatment of
diffusion-based molecular communication across heterogeneous discrete environments.
